Why Students Choose to Study in Greece?

About Greece

Greece, the cradle of Western civilization, is renowned for its ancient history, beautiful islands, and vibrant student life. Over the years, it has also become a compelling study destination for international students, offering affordable, high-quality education with programs taught in both Greek and English. Greek universities are known for their strong academic foundations, especially in philosophy, medicine, architecture, and engineering. The warm Mediterranean climate, rich culture, and relatively low cost of living make Greece an ideal place to live and study.

Why Study in Greece?

Greece combines academic excellence with a rich historical and cultural experience. Many public universities offer low or no tuition fees, especially for EU students, and affordable options for non-EU students. A growing number of programs, particularly at the Master's level, are now available in English. With a strong emphasis on research, critical thinking, and theoretical grounding, Greek universities prepare students for both local and global careers. The welcoming lifestyle, affordable living, and student-friendly cities enhance the overall study experience.

Greece Study Visa Requirements

Non-EU students must apply for a National Type D Visa to study in Greece. Here are the main requirements:

  • Letter of admission from a recognized Greek university
  • Valid passport
  • Proof of financial means (~‚Ç¨600/month)
  • Health insurance valid in Greece
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Academic documents (translated into Greek if required)
  • Police clearance certificate
  • Visa application form and fee

Note: Applications must be submitted to the Greek embassy or consulate in the student‚Äôs home country.

Post-Study Work Permit (PSW) in Greece

While Greece does not have a formal post-study work visa scheme like some other countries, graduates can apply to extend their residence permit if they find employment or enroll in further education. Employment in Greece may lead to a residence/work permit, which can later be converted into a long-term or permanent residence status. Being an EU member, Greece also offers potential mobility to other EU countries for further job opportunities.

Tip: Start networking and job hunting 3-6 months before graduation for smoother transition.
